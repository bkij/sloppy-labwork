About Deck Registration

Many online tournaments ask players to prove they have access to a deck before using it in an event. Usually this involves taking a picture of your deck's archon card alongside a handwritten note.

The registration service here aims to streamline that process for players and tournament organizers. Players get a consistent verification experience, and organizers get less overhead and can focus on running their event.

Goals

Our goals for this service are pretty simple:

  • Streamline event sign-up for player and organizers.
  • Make it easy to share decks with your friends, teammates, and community.

We believe sharing is good and want to put tools in the hands of event organizers that make sharing as frictionless as possible.

How It Works, For Players

Registering a deck with Sloppy Labwork will feel similar to deck verification processes you might have experienced with online events in the past. After creating an account, you can submit a registration request here. Your registration request will include a link to your deck as well as a photo of your deck and a handwritten note.

Before, you might have included contact information and a date in verification photos. Instead, we generate random verification codes for each request. This removes the need to have a date in your photo - we know the exact time each code is generated. Similarly, these codes are tied to the Sloppy Labwork account that submitted the deck registration request. Registration requests will be associated with the contact information in your account, even if you update that information later.

When a registration request is submitted, Sloppy Labwork will attempt to parse your deck's name and a handwritten verification code from the photo automatically. When that happens, your request may be approved within seconds of the time it was submitted. If we're unable to verify your request automatically, it will then be reviewed by a Sloppy Labwork administrator. Once approved, you will be able to sign up for events using that deck.

In a rush? If we aren't able to verify your first request automatically you may submit a new request for the same deck and we'll archive your previous attempt. Within a single day you may submit up to ten registration requests.

Here are some tips for ensuring your verification photo can be processed automatically:

  • Your photo should show the back of any card from your deck. If you see a QR code (for archon cards) or can read the card's name, flip it over.
  • Use a card other than the archon card. Every card in your deck has its name printed on the back. We prefer not to use archon cards as those also have house names spelled out.
  • If your deck is still sealed, you do not need to unwrap it. See the example photo below.
  • The deck name should be fully visible, in focus, and with minimal glare. Be mindful of glare obscuring your deck's name when it is still in cellophane
  • Make sure there is no other writing or text in your image. Other text might confuse our automated process.
  • Use a plain background with no markings.
