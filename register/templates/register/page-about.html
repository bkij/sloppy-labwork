About Deck Registration

Many online tournaments ask players to prove they have access to a deck before using it in an event. Usually this involves taking a picture of your deck's archon card alongside a handwritten note.

The registration service here aims to streamline that process for players and tournament organizers. Players get a consistent verification experience, and organizers get less overhead and can focus on running their event.

Goals

Our goals for this service are pretty simple:

  • Streamline event sign-up for player and event organizers.
  • Make it easy to share decks with your friends, teammates, and community.

Deck sharing and netdecking can be a contentious topic. We come down pretty firmly on the side of "sharing is good"™ and want to put tools in the hands of event organizers that make sharing easy.

How It Works, For Players

Registering a deck with Sloppy Labwork will feel similar to deck verification processes you might have gone through for online events in the past. After creating an account, you can submit a registration request here. Your registration request will include a photo of your archon card, as well as a handwritten note containing a randomly generated 5-digit code specific to your registration request.

Before you might have included your contact information and a date in verification photos. Instead, we generate random verification codes for each request. This removes the need to have a date in your photo - we know the exact time each 5-digit code is generated. Similarly, these codes are tied to the Sloppy Labwork account that submitted the deck registration request. The registration request will be connected to the contact information in your account, even if you later update that information.

When a verification request is submitted, Sloppy Labwork will attempt to parse your deck's name and handwritten verification code from the photo automatically. When that happens, your request may be approved within seconds of the time it was submitted. If we're unable to verify your request automatically, it will then be reviewed by a Sloppy Labwork administrator. Once approved, you will be able to sign up for events with that deck.

Here are some tips to ensuring your verification photo can be processed automatically:

  • The back of your archon card should be face up in your photo. This is the side with the archon artwork, not the QR code side. If you can read your deck list, flip the card over.
  • The deck name should be fully visible, in focus, and with minimal glare. This is only part of the card that need visible fore, verification. In particular, be mindful of glare obscuring your deck's name, especially if it is still in cellophane
  • Make sure there is no other writing or text in your image. Other text will potentially confuse the automated process.

Keep in mind that, after verification, your photo will be visible to others who would like to double check your claim.
